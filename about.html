About Us

A brand new collaboration between two individuals with a shared passion for programming and machine learning, InterData Innovation Labs (i2Labs) starts out with a mission to make programming for young students accessible and relevant.

We believe that the fundamental skills required for programming starts from young. By making subjects learnt in school relevant to programming, concepts become self-reinforcing and less intimidating. We aim to break down notions that programming is a niche and difficult subject to learn. With extensive real-world experience in programming, we become not just instructors but also mentors for higher education and career advice.

Currently we want to reach out to schools to provide trial classes during the school holidays. These classes introduce simple programming concepts. Students learn how to code 2D games using Racket/Scheme, which is taught to first year students in many Universities and the language used in the famous SICP textbook by MIT. By recreating common games such as Snake and Tetris, classes aim to be engaging and fun especially since it is the holidays! You can find out more about our lesson plans in our blog.

Features

Future-proof learning

A focus on programming principles rather than language, thus building the foundation for learning future languages and being adaptable.

Closely aligned with school math syllabus

Incorporating concepts learnt in school into programming exercises, reinforcing theoretical knowledge and exposure to real-world applications and best industry practices.

Engaging projects that can be explored at home

Simple setup allows kids to continue exploring at home with minimal setup cost. Fun projects such as game design to keep students interested.
